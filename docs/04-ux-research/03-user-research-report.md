# User Research Report - Báo Cáo Nghiên Cứu Người Dùng

---

## 1. Research Overview

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Research Period | Q1/2026 |
| Methodology | Mixed Methods (Quant + Qual) |
| Participants | 150 survey + 30 interviews |
| Target Group | Adults 22-55, urban VN, interested in insurance |
| Researcher | UX Research Team |

---

## 2. Research Objectives

1. Validate demand for online insurance purchase in Vietnam
2. Understand current pain points with insurance buying process
3. Identify key decision factors when choosing insurance
4. Determine preferred digital channels and UX expectations
5. Map the emotional journey of insurance buyers
6. Understand barriers to online insurance adoption

---

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1. Quantitative Research
- **Online Survey**: 150 respondents
- **Distribution**: Facebook ads, Zalo groups, email lists
- **Demographics**: 22-55 years, income > 10M VND/month, urban (HCM + HN)
- **Survey Length**: 25 questions, ~8 minutes
- **Incentive**: 50K VND voucher

### 3.2. Qualitative Research
- **In-depth Interviews**: 30 participants (60 min each)
- **Format**: Video call (Zoom) + screen share for prototype testing
- **Segments**: 8 young professionals, 8 parents, 7 SME owners, 7 first-timers
- **Questions**: Semi-structured, open-ended
- **Analysis**: Thematic coding, affinity mapping

### 3.3. Competitive Analysis
- **Platforms reviewed**: 8 (Bảo Việt, PVI, Saladin, Liberty, Tiki, MoMo Insurance, etc.)
- **Evaluation**: UX audit, feature comparison, user reviews analysis
- **Source**: App stores, social media, mystery shopping

## 4. Key Findings

### 4.1. Finding 1: Strong Demand for Online Insurance

> **78% of respondents said they would buy insurance online if they trusted the platform**

| Willingness to Buy Online | Percentage |
|---------------------------|-----------|
| Definitely yes | 32% |
| Probably yes | 46% |
| Maybe | 15% |
| Probably no | 5% |
| Definitely no | 2% |

**Key Insight**: Trust is the main barrier, not willingness. Build trust signals.

## 5. Segment-Specific Insights

### 5.1. Young Professionals (22-30)

| Insight | Implication |
|---------|------------|
| 90% use mobile primarily | Mobile-first design mandatory |
| Average session: 3-5 minutes | Quick flows, minimal steps |
| Trust influencer reviews > official ads | Influencer marketing strategy |
| Prefer self-service, avoid phone calls | Chatbot + in-app help |
| Price-sensitive, compare extensively | Comparison tool is key |
| 65% have NO insurance currently | Education + simple starter products |

### 5.2. Parents (30-45)

| Insight | Implication |
|---------|------------|
| #1 concern: Children's health protection | Family plan prominence |
| Research extensively before buying | Detailed comparison, reviews |
| Discuss with spouse before purchase | Share/save features |
| Trust reviews from other parents | Community reviews section |
| Willing to pay more for better coverage | Upsell premium plans |
| 80% already have some insurance | Cross-sell, upgrade path |

### 5.3. SME Owners (35-55)

| Insight | Implication |
|---------|------------|
| Time is #1 constraint | Quick quote, account manager |
| Need corporate invoicing | Tax invoice generation |
| Want to manage employee benefits | HR portal, bulk management |
| Prefer desktop for work tasks | Desktop-optimized admin UI |
| High-value contracts (200M+ VND/year) | Dedicated sales support |
| Decision often involves multiple people | Multi-user access, approval flow |

### 7.3. Design Principles (Derived from Research)

1. **Transparency over persuasion** - Show exclusions as clearly as benefits
2. **Speed over completeness** - Get to price fast, details on demand
3. **Compare over describe** - Users decide by comparison
4. **Educate over sell** - Build trust through knowledge
5. **Mobile over desktop** - 76% will use mobile
6. **Visual over text** - Use icons, progress bars, timelines
